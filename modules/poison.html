Curation checks incoming samples against the rest of the data and rejects outliers — it catches the crude, obvious poison, and the boundary snaps back toward clean.

But the backdoor's trigger zone doesn't come from a "weird" training point that stands out. It comes from a consistent, deliberate bad source. Detecting anomalies after the fact isn't the same as knowing where your data came from in the first place. That's provenance — and it's a governance control, not a filter.

A POISONED MODEL BETRAYS ITS OWNER.

But even a clean model has a secret it can betray — the private data it learned from.
