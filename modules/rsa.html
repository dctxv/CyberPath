SECRECY WITHOUT A SHARED SECRET.

You sent a secret to a stranger with nothing agreed in advance. But when you grabbed the server's public key off the open wire — how do you know it was really theirs, and not the eavesdropper's lock swapped in? You solved secrecy. You have NOT solved identity.
