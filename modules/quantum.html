SCAN 03 // THE RIGGED GAME

DON'T CHECK BOTH. CANCEL THE WRONG ONE.

A hidden setting to discover. Classically you probe and get a coin-flip of info each time. Quantumly you arrange the wrong answer to cancel — then one measurement is enough.

WRONG ANSWER: AMPLITUDE 0 // RIGHT ANSWER: AMPLITUDE ~1 // SOLVED IN 1 MEASUREMENT. You didn't check both settings — you made the wrong one cancel, so the right one was all that was left to measure. That is what a quantum computer does.

NOT "TRIES EVERYTHING." MAKES THE RIGHT ANSWER LOUD.

For most problems that buys you nothing. But for a few specific ones — one being the exact math RSA hides behind — it changes everything. Want to point it at the lock you built?

HONESTY MANDATE // Amplitudes are "arrows that can cancel," not probabilities — probabilities never cancel, and that is the whole distinction. This does NOT claim "tries all answers at once," "parallel universes computing," or "exponential speedup for everything" — those are the common myths. Real qubits live on a sphere (the Bloch sphere) and real algorithms use many linked qubits; this arrow is the honest 1-qubit shadow.
