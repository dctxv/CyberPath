THE SOBER PART.

Quantum computers big enough to do this to real keys don't exist yet — but they might, and data stolen today could be cracked later ("harvest now, decrypt later"). So the world is already rebuilding the lock on a different hard problem — one where interference doesn't help the attacker.
HONESTY MANDATE // Real quantum computers CANNOT yet factor RSA-sized numbers — this demonstrates the principle, and "harvest now, decrypt later" is why it matters despite that. RSA is not broken today. Shor's is period-finding via interference (cancel wrong periods), NOT "tries all factors at once." Note: quantum only weakens symmetric keys like AES (double the key size), it does not shatter them the way it does RSA.
