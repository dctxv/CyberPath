CHAPTER 00 // WHY A NEW LOCK
  1. 01RSA hid a repeating pattern. A quantum computer is a machine for finding repeating patterns — so it finds RSA's, and the lock falls.
  2. 02This new lock hides a location instead — one exact dot in a huge grid of dots. There's no pattern to repeat, so there's nothing for the quantum machine to lock onto.
  3. 03Everything ahead is that one idea. The grid dots are all the possible locked messages; the ◆ is YOUR message, knocked just off a dot by deliberate noise. The right key knows which dot it came from. A wrong key only guesses.

BUILT TO SURVIVE WHAT BROKE THE LAST ONE.

RSA bet its life on a problem with a hidden pattern, and quantum found the pattern. This lock bets on geometry: a closest-point problem that gets brutal as dimension rises, with no known efficient quantum shortcut.

HONESTY MANDATE // The 2D grid is the honest shadow of real lattices with hundreds of dimensions, where human intuition fails entirely. "Quantum has no advantage" means no KNOWN efficient quantum attack — the honest state of the field, not "provably impossible forever." This is real and current: lattice-based schemes are being standardized right now to replace RSA.
