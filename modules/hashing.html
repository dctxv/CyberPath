A hash is a meat-grinder for data. Whatever you feed it — a word, a whole book — falls through and comes out as one fixed-size fingerprint. Feed it something and watch.

SAME MESSAGE, ONE LETTER MOVED // FINGERPRINT DELTA / {{ haAvalanche }}% OF THE PRINT FLIPPED. A tiny lie makes a totally different fingerprint — that is what makes it a tamper detector.
JAMMED // A FINGERPRINT REVEALS NOTHING ABOUT ITS INPUT. The grinder has no reverse. That is why passwords are stored as hashes — steal the prints, you still can't get the passwords back.

IT CATCHES ANY CHANGE.

But a fingerprint can't tell you WHO sent a message — an attacker could change it and grind a fresh print. You need a fingerprint only one specific person could have produced. Hold that thought.
